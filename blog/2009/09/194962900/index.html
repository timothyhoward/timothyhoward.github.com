Cabel says it well:

Can I just stop here, and can we be honest with each other? Does this kind of acting resonate with anybody? […] Are there people who can relate to — let alone enjoy — this video? And has there ever been a more vibrant and tangible demonstration of the difference between Apple and Microsoft?

At first I thought I’d need to be careful to avoid overstating my case as what’s wrong with this video (and on a higher level, the entire concept) should be crystal clear to everyone. But obviously it’s not, this and the other 100+ supporting videos didn’t make themselves. Real people had to pitch, write, storyboard, film, direct, edit, publish and sign off on this series. Having read many stories about the bureaucracy involved in writing software for Microsoft surely that same bureaucracy should stop videos like this ever reaching the public eye.

The concept is insane. I love OS X and there is no way I would ever host a “launch party” for a new release, no matter what the incentive. Who the fuck are they targetting with this campaign? Who in their right mind invites friends into their homes to promote a product they have absolutely no stake in? The people in these commercials don’t exist, the way they’re acting and talking is down right creepy.

Here’s another strange, completely unrealistic marketing video for Microsoft Songsmith.

What is wrong with Microsoft? As much as you might like Windows, is software made by the same people who invest resources into embarrassingly out-of-touch marketing campaigns like these something anyone can get behind? Like finding out your favourite football player is into dog fighting, they’re unrelated but you can’t ignore it.
