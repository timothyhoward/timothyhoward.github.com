I refuse to believe that the Gruen Transfer helps retail in any way. The kind of store that tends to stock the clothes I like, department stores, are notoriously the most “effective” implementors of the Gruen Transfer.

They scatter their clothes throughout a huge area. Shorts, T-shirts, collared shirts and jackets are all dispersed over 50mÂ². It’s impossible to compare the range and you’re forced to walk around.

When you finally find the clothes you want, you then have to find where to pay for it.

Surely frustrating your shoppers by forcing them to search for similar items and a place to pay for them couldn’t possibly outweigh helping shoppers find what they want and pay for it as efficiently as possible.

I imagine department store executives: “I know! We’ll disorient our customers, make it difficult to browse our entire range and force them search for the place where they give us their money!”
