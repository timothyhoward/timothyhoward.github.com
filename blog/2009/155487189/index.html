The Ride to Cure Diabetes

Early next year I’m taking part in the Ride to Cure Diabetes. It’s a 160km ride through the hills of the Barossa Valley in South Australia. To be eligible to participate I need to raise $3500. I’ve setup a website to collect donations, chart my progress, explain what Juvenile Diabetes is, why I’m doing it and why you should donate.

My sister was diagnosed with Type 1 Diabetes when she was 12 years old and I’ve seen first hand how it can affect a young person’s life. Type 1 Diabetes is treatable but it’s a heavy burden for a child to hold when the complications can be so severe. The Juvenile Diabetes Research Foundation, the charity behind the ride to cure, are solely dedicated to finding a cure for Type 1 Diabetes. They don’t waste money on “awareness raising”, every donation goes towards the research that will eventually find a cure.

While the main purpose of riding is to raise funds for JDRF, recovering from my bike accident a little over a month ago is secondary. 160km through hilly terrain is an intimidating thought while my bike is crushed and 18 of my bones remain broken but it’s a goal that will only aid my recovery.

Donations are handled through my page at JDRF but you can find more information and a cool graph I’ll be continually updating at my own fundraising website. I’ll be hugely grateful for donations of any amount. If you run a business and would like a sponsorship arrangement I’m open to anything, contact me at jimwhimpey@me.com. Thank you!
