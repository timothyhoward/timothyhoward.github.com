Losing Everything

No matter where you are in the world you’ve probably heard about the devastation of the Victorian Bushfires over the last few weeks. Over two hundred people lost their lives and thousands more lost everything they owned.

Today was a national day of mourning and my girlfriend and I were talking to her Mum about the loss. She couldn’t believe how people could deal with losing everything.

A bit of back story. My girlfriend has a huge family that’s lived in the same huge house on a huge block of land for the last 19 years. They keep everything.

As long as you were fully insured, I’d see it less as loss and more of a fresh start. As much as I think about it, I can’t think of a possession I own that couldn’t be replaced by money. But what about the photographs?! All the best are hosted at Flickr and the rest are backed up at MobileMe. Any printed photographs I own originated as digital files I’ll still have.

I’ve found a lot of people have sentimental and emotional attachments to material things: cars, furniture, computers, phones. When I sold my piece of shit car, my Mum asked if I said good bye to it. No, I didn’t, because it’s just a mass produced hunk of metal and plastic.

Already someone who doesn’t believe in sentimental value, who throws away anything that has no practical use, spending four months overseas living out of a single backpack made it clear how easy it was to live with even less. While the shock of “losing everything you’ve worked your whole life to attain” would be great at first, I’m sure many of the bushfire victims who’s friends, family and pets are still alive will soon realise how little they’ve lost in the big scheme of things.

Feb 22nd, 2009
