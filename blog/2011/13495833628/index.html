Overworking Culture

In a typical case of assholery, Mike Arrington misappropriated Jamie Zawinski’s comments from over 15 years ago to support his gross, self-serving argument for startup employees working themselves into the ground.

Delightfully, Jamie responded:

What is true is that for a VC’s business model to work, it’s necessary for you to give up your life in order for him to become richer.

At the end of Arrington’s diatribe he links to this NY Times post titled, Zyngaâ€™s Tough Culture Risks a Talent Drain with the comment: “FFS, the place has free acupuncture and an organic cafeteria” as though that somehow makes up for… urgh, I can’t even respond.

I’ve never used any of Zynga’s stuff so I can’t comment on the result first hand but I think the fourth comment on that article unintentionally summarises the situation beautifully:

Zynga’s software is terrible. Their programmers don’t deserve their stock options. How is it possible that all those hard charging long hours add up to the most dysfunctional experience on the internet?

Good question.

The answer is that a quality result not only has little to do with raw hours but is often inversely proportional to the amount of hours spent on something beyond what allows a balanced life. The currency of a developer is brain power, not brain time. The kind of conditions Arrington is advocating is like suggesting a terrible song would be much more popular if only it went for twice as long.
