About Me

Hello, I’m Tim. I am a 20-year-old Bachelor of Arts undergraduate at The University of Melbourne, majoring in Political Science and Islamic Studies. My passion lies within the realm of African and Islamic Politics, although I also have a keen interest in Australian and American Politics, as well as International Law, History, Technology, Photography, and Culture & Design. Away from academia, I enjoy reading and listening to music, supporting Liverpool FC in the English Premier League, and participating in social justice causes, having previously travelled to Papua New Guinea and Southern India to build houses and accommodation for disadvantaged students. I aspire to one day pursue a career in diplomacy, helping to tackle problems of poverty and discrimination within developing nation-states.

My weblog is a medium to share with the world exactly what is on my mind. I love to learn new things, and with the world changing so quickly – especially in the political sphere – there’s always something new to learn. I hope to write long-form essays concerning a wide-range of topics that interest me, spanning topics from the past and present. I also hope to engage in debate with people using the comments system, so if you feel like writing a response to an article I write, please do – I appreciate all sides of the debate, whether you agree with me or take an alternative viewpoint. I do hope that you enjoy following my weblog, thanks for having a look!
